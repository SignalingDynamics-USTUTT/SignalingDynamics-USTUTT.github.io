      <strong>Self-Introduction</strong>
      <p>I have taken a highly interdisciplinary career path. Initially trained as a theoretical chemist, I switched to the world of biology in my graduate studies and build mathematical models to explain how biological noises lead to heterogeneous cell phenotypes. Feeling that I lacked first-hand experience in the wet lab, I then joined an experimental lab in my postdoc, where I spent half of my time building computational infrastructure for the lab and the other half performing cell-biology and imaging experiments by myself. Co-incidentally, this career path gives me rigorous training in most aspects of systems biology. Along the path, I have published many first- and co-first-author papers in internationally reputable journals, such as Nature Communications, Cell Reports, eLife, and Nucleic Acids Research, and all of my papers consist of significant experimental and computational aspects. As a Junior Professor, I wish that my training could drive me to unravel why cancer therapies fail.</p>
